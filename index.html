Our first date was at Biercraft, a Belgian pub on Commerical Street in East Vancouver. Amanda had said that she was into craft beers so it seemed like a good bet. After a couple beers, hours of politely declining on the last mussel and last of the fries (even though we were still both mad hungry but didn't want to come across as savages) then chatting until the lights came on and they closed up shop - TN

THE RING

So for the ring I [Tony] wanted to find something that was uniquely Amanda, but after doing weeks of research I couldn't find anyting that would release methane gas after every meal and kick me in the middle of the night. Eventually I found a place in East Van that had very 'West Coast' / 'Pacific Northwest' style. I spent hours with the ring designer sketching out designs and inspecting diamonds and ended up with an awesome design that was unique and very 'Amanda'. First person to see it was Mr Robert Q. (future Father-in-Law) Lee, then after a bit of awkward conversation asking for his daughter's hand in marriage, tidbits of marriage advice, followed by adequate libations, and of course getting the thumbs up - I was off to figure out the proposal thing! - TN

01
01.17

THE PROPOSAL

Just before Christmas Tony suggested that we go away for New Years Eve, even going as far as choosing the destination (Salt Spring Island), making the AirBnB booking and planning the ferry schedule. HIGHLY SUSPICIOUS! But after no proposal on NYE I thought my spidey-proposal senses were all off. On January 1st we woke up to a sunny and crisp morning and Tony suggested that we enjoy our morning coffee on the patio and to open my 'late' Christmas present that he toted all this way. To my dismay when I opened the box I saw an iPhone 7 box and i'm pretty sure I asked: "Why did you get me this, I don't need a new phone", but he urged me to open the box and when I did he was already down on one knee. The next 60 seconds were a bit of a blur because I was too busy crying to hear any of the speech that he had prepared. Onesie-clad and a bad case of bedhead, we were engaged, and it was perfect! - AL

TEA CEREMONY

In Chinese and Vietnamese culture the tea ceremony is a way to show respect to parents, family, and friends for their love and support through their life. The purity of tea represents love that is pure and noble; stability of tea stands for faithful love; fertility of tea represents many future children.
